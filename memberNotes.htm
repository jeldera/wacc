Member Notes

W.A.R.M. – ROTARY HOLIDAY FOOD DRIVE

The Rotary Clubs of Westerville, the Otterbein Rotaract Club members, Otterbein alumni, the High School Interact Clubs, Boy Scouts & WARM volunteers will be collecting canned food donations for those in need again this holiday season. W.A.R.M.’s Food Pantry is always in need of cereal, canned meats (tuna, chicken, etc.) spaghetti sauce and side dishes such as pastas, rice and potatoes. At the annual Westerville Holiday Tree Lighting ceremony on December 2nd at City Hall, volunteers will be on hand to collect canned goods and donations. On December 10th the service groups will be collecting in areas of the city as well as accepting donations of non-perishable food and cash at three drop-off locations: W.A.R.M. central office at 175 East Broadway and at both Westerville area Kroger stores on E. Schrock Road and Maxton Road locations. The drop-off locations will be open from 9:00am to 12:00noon. This community wide event is supported by Kroger, Tim Horton’s, The Westerville Area Chamber and the City of Westerville Parks & Rec. Department.

HEART OF CONCORD

Join Concord Counseling Services for the “HeArt of Concord” on Thursday, November 3 from 5:00 p.m-7:00 p.m. at 700 Brooksedge Blvd., Westerville, 43081. The event is free and open to the public. Clients and friends of Concord will be displaying paintings, photographs, drawings, fabric art, jewelry, pottery, etc. Guests will be invited to add their own expression of “What being mentally healthy (or not) feels like” to a continually evolving collage. Partners include: Gallery 202, Partners in Art, Inc. and FastSigns-Westerville. For more information, please visit www.concordcounseling.org; email us, or visit the Heart Facebook page.

WESTERVILLE CHRISTIAN CHURCH – THE GIFT SHOP

The Gift Shop....Think outside the gift box. We want you to be a part of something great, the opportunity to help local families have a memorable and joyous Christmas. This December, Westerville Christian Church is committed to bringing hope to thirty-five local families through the Gift Shop. How can you help? The Gift Shop will be hosted by Westerville Christian Church, 471 East College Ave, on December 2.  Working with the Westerville City Schools, we are collecting gifts for families in need.  You can help by providing items in the following categories: Board games and Books (children and adults); Outerwear (children and adults; ex. coats, hats, scarves, gloves); Small Electronics/Appliances (ex. clock radio, mp3 player, handheld video games, camera, toaster, crock-pot etc.); Toys (ex. stuffed animals, dolls, action figures, coloring books, crayons, craft kits, etc.); Sporting Goods (ex. bats, balls, corn hole, scooters, jump ropes, etc.); Monetary offering (to purchase items). We hope you will consider helping us fill the shop this year. For more information, please contact Leslie Bodkin at 614-578-7764 to make arrangements.

VETERANS 5K WALK/RUN

The Rotary Club of Westerville (noon) is putting together the inaugural Rotary Honors Veterans 5K Walk/Run for this November 5 at 9am at Alum Creek Park (7:45a.m. registration). This is the week prior to Veteran's Day. As you know, Rotary is about "Service above self" and we are using this event to honor those that have made the ultimate commitment of Service above self to their country. The cost to participate in the 5K after October 30th is $35 per person. Kids can also participate at $15 in the Kids Fun Run. All pre-registered kids will receive a race shirt and finishers medal, with a few other goodies! For more information, please contact us.
